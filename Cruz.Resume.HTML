    Field Trip: Workday Visit

    Thanks to my Engineering Academy, I was able to visit the company Workday to look at different aspects of a physical workspace that concerned user management and design. I have two cousins that are beginning to work at Workday this year and I chose to go on this field trip because I wanted to see what exactly took place in a working environment. This field trip had greatly helped me in my college major selection process, since I was debating over choosing majoring in Physics or Computer Science as a major that I would want to choose. After this trip, I began to lean more towards choosing computer science, because it would open up opportunities for me to work in a place similar to Workday.

    Field Trip: Google Campus

    This field trip is one that will remain important to me forever since it served as a great source of motivation for me to work harder in high school and also to show me different parts of the environment in Google. I had always wondered what it would be like working at Google, and now that I have committed to UC Santa Cruz, I can have the chance to visit Google more and attempt to network with individuals there. Seeing the different aspects of the Google Campus showed me how vast and beautiful the workspace I had dreamed of could be, and made me want to work at Google even more in the future.

    Guest Speaker: James Morehead

    One of the most notable guest speakers I listened to was James Morehead. He works at Google as a product manager and most notably worked on a part of Google called "Google Bulletin". This service, Bulletin, allows users to post different kinds of real-life content, similar to Instagram and Snapchat, but have their content globally broadcasted and made very easily accessible to people. He spoke a lot about the process of starting a project like Bulletin at a huge company in Google, and opened my eyes to realize the amount of time and effort managers and developers must put into creating new ideas for people.

    Guest Speaker: Elyse Chen

    Ms. Chen is a researcher from LAM Research who was the first guest speaker I listened to in the 2018-2019 school year. She taught us a lot about how microchips are designed and produced. Her speech went in-depth about the different steps of creating the chips and components of the hardware we use today. I found her speaking to be very interesting because I had always thought about how the motherboards in computers looked so pretty and intricate, and this lecture showed me how those objects of my fascination came to be.

    Mentorship Program 2017-2018

    In my junior year of high school, I was assigned a mentor to help me in my school life and life proceeding towards a career. His name is Dayne Bratsman. Right now, he is a product manager at Coursehero, and I have learned so much from the small amounts of time that we were able to spend with each other.

    He was an amazing mentor. He was a person I was able to speak well to and listened well to everything I said and offered so much solid advice on all of my interests and needs. He taught me to think differently about different aspects of programming and mathematics in school, and also helped me improve the way that I allocate my time outside of school. However, one of my most memorable conversations with him was the way he emphasized the different aspects of prospective employees for a company. In front of me, he compared two resumes of prospective employees. One employee showed a very high GPA in college, while the other showed a less high GPA that had more experience outside the classroom in internships and in pursuit of goals through projects. He helped me reinforce the fact in my mind that I would not just need good grades to succeed, but to apply concepts and information that I have learned to real-world projects. He told me that it was experience that matters the most, and in turn I have now been thinking of ways I can use my skills I have gained in programming and engineering these four years to provide assistance to the less fortunate around me and in preparation for the workplace.
